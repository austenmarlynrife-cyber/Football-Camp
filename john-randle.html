John Randle

John Randle played college football at Trinity Valley Community College and Texas A&I (now Texas A&M-Kingsville) before entering the NFL as an undrafted free agent in 1990.

After signing with Minnesota following the 1990 draft, Randle became one of the most dominant defensive tackles of his era with the Vikings and Seahawks.

Major accolades include 7 Pro Bowls, 6 First-Team All-Pro selections, NFL sacks leader (1997), NFL 1990s All-Decade Team, and induction into the Pro Football Hall of Fame (2010).

Career stats: 219 games played, 137.5 sacks, 471 combined tackles, 29 forced fumbles, and 11 fumble recoveries.
